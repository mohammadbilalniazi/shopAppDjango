Financial Reports Module Documentation

1. Module Scope

This module provides financial reporting for organizations, including balance sheet, profit and loss, cash flow, and inter-organization ledger summaries. It is designed to support both operational dashboards and audit-style ledger review for organizations that manage cross-organization bills and payments.

Primary files:
- asset/models.py
- asset/utils.py
- asset/views.py
- asset/views_financial.py
- bill/models.py
- shop/urls.py

2. Core Financial Models

OrganizationAsset
Tracks current financial position for an organization, including assets, liabilities, equity, and P&L items. It is automatically recalculated from bill activity.

Key fields:
- inventory_value: inventory asset value
- cash_on_hand: liquid cash asset
- accounts_receivable: money owed to the organization
- accounts_payable: money owed by the organization
- loans_receivable: money lent out
- loans_payable: money borrowed
- total_assets: sum of inventory, cash, receivables, loans receivable
- total_liabilities: sum of payables and loans payable
- equity: total_assets - total_liabilities
- total_revenue: revenue from SELLING bills
- total_cost_of_goods_sold: cost from PURCHASE bills
- total_expenses: total from EXPENSE bills
- total_losses: total from LOSSDEGRADE bills
- net_profit: revenue - COGS - expenses - losses

AssetWholeBillSummary
Provides cached aggregate data by organization and bill type, used by the financial summary dashboard for performance.

Key fields:
- bill_type: PURCHASE, SELLING, PAYMENT, RECEIVEMENT, EXPENSE, LOSSDEGRADE
- total: total billed amount
- payment: sum of payments applied
- profit: derived or stored profit amount

LedgerAdjustment
Manual ledger correction records stored in bill/models.py. These adjustments allow administrators to correct running inter-organization balances when bills or payments are reconciled outside the automated flow.

3. Balance Sheet Reporting

The balance sheet is generated by asset/utils.py:get_balance_sheet(organization). It returns:
- assets.current_assets: cash, accounts_receivable, inventory, loans_receivable, total
- assets.total_assets
- liabilities.current_liabilities: accounts_payable, loans_payable, total
- liabilities.total_liabilities
- equity.retained_earnings: net_profit
- equity.total_equity

Calculation behavior:
- If OrganizationAsset does not exist, the module recalculates it by aggregating bills.
- Total assets and liabilities are kept in sync with the organization asset summary.

4. Profit and Loss Statement

The P&L statement is generated by asset/utils.py:get_profit_loss_statement(organization). It returns:
- revenue.total_revenue
- cost_of_sales.total_cogs
- gross_profit = revenue - cost_of_sales
- operating_expenses.total_expenses
- operating_expenses.losses_from_damage
- net_profit

This statement is derived from the cached OrganizationAsset values, which are kept up to date by bill and asset processing.

5. Cash Flow Summary

The cash flow summary is generated by asset/utils.py:get_cash_flow_summary(organization). It segregates cash activity into:
- operating_activities: cash from sales, cash for purchases, cash for expenses, cash for losses, net operating cash
- financing_activities: cash received, cash paid, net financing cash
- net_cash_flow: operating + financing cash flow

Bill types used:
- SELLING for cash from sales
- PURCHASE for cash payments on purchases
- EXPENSE for operating cash outflow
- LOSSDEGRADE for loss-related outflow
- RECEIVEMENT for cash received from other parties
- PAYMENT for cash paid to other parties

6. Organization Ledger Summary

The ledger summary view, asset/views_financial.py:organization_ledger_summary, supports two modes:
- summary mode: selected organization vs all counterpart organizations
- detail mode: selected organization vs a specific opposite organization

When both organizations are selected, the ledger combines:
- bills between the two organizations
- manual LedgerAdjustment entries
- a running balance computed using signed bill impact

Bill impact rules:
- SELLING: customer owes org more (total - payment)
- PURCHASE: org has paid more than owed (payment - total)
- PAYMENT: reduces org debt by payment amount
- RECEIVEMENT: reduces counterpart debt by payment amount

7. Financial Dashboard

The dashboard view, asset/views_financial.py:financial_summary_dashboard, uses AssetWholeBillSummary for fast aggregation. It calculates:
- total_revenue from SELLING
- total_cost from PURCHASE
- total_expenses from EXPENSE
- total_losses from LOSSDEGRADE
- gross_profit = revenue - cost
- net_profit = gross_profit - expenses - losses
- accounts_receivable = outstanding SELLING amount
- accounts_payable = outstanding PURCHASE amount
- loans_receivable = outstanding PAYMENT amount
- loans_payable = outstanding RECEIVEMENT amount
- net_position = receivables - payables + loans_receivable - loans_payable

8. Routes and Access

Key routes are registered in asset/urls.py and shop/urls.py under the asset/financial reports section:
- /balance-sheet/ → asset.views.balance_sheet_view
- /profit-loss/ → asset.views.profit_loss_view
- /cash-flow/ → asset.views.cash_flow_view
- /ledger/ → asset.views_financial.organization_ledger_summary
- /ledger/adjustment/save/ → asset.views_financial.ledger_adjustment_save
- /financial/ → asset.views_financial.financial_summary_dashboard

Access control:
- All financial pages require login.
- Ledger adjustments require authenticated users and enforce org membership checks for non-admin users.
- Admin-level summary adjustments require staff permissions.

9. Thesis and Reporting Notes

This module is designed for thesis documentation of supermarket electronic management because it links operational billing to accounting-style financial statements. It supports both live dashboards and ledger reconciliation scenarios, making it suitable for discussions about data denormalization, performance-sensitive reporting, and multi-organization financial control.
