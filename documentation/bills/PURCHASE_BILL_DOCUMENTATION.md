Purchase Bill Documentation

1. Purpose

PURCHASE bills represent goods acquired by an organization from a supplier or another organization. In the supermarket system, a PURCHASE bill increases stock and creates accounts payable until payment is made.

2. Key Characteristics

- bill_type: PURCHASE
- creator organization: purchaser
- receiver organization: supplier or counterparty organization when inter-organization billing is used
- inventory effect: stock quantity increases
- cost effect: purchased_price updated on product details
- payment effect: payment amount may be partial or full, creating outstanding payables

3. Opposite Transaction

PURCHASE bills are paired with SELLING bills for the supplier organization. This pairing supports consistent recording of asset movement and cost between two organizations.

4. Financial Impact

- cost of goods sold is driven by purchase cost on the buying side
- accounts payable grows by outstanding amount (total − payment)
- inventory assets increase with purchased quantity
- cash flow is impacted when payment is made to settle the bill

5. Thesis Relevance

For thesis writing, PURCHASE bills demonstrate:
- supplier-side cost recognition in retail procurement
- payables management and working capital effects
- the link between purchase events and balance sheet liabilities
- how purchase entries feed the cash flow and profit-loss structure
