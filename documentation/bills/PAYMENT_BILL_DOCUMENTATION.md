Payment Bill Documentation

1. Purpose

PAYMENT bills represent cash or financial payments made by an organization to another organization. They typically settle outstanding purchase invoices, expenses, or loan obligations.

2. Key Characteristics

- bill_type: PAYMENT
- creator organization: payer
- receiver organization: payee organization
- cash flow effect: reduces liquid cash asset for the payer
- ledger effect: decreases the payer's outstanding debt to the recipient

3. Opposite Transaction

PAYMENT bills are paired with RECEIVEMENT bills for the recipient organization. This ensures that the paying organization and receiving organization both record the same cash movement.

4. Financial Impact

- financing cash flow decreases for the payer organization
- accounts payable or other liabilities are reduced when the payment is applied
- the payer's net cash position decreases by the payment amount

5. Thesis Relevance

PAYMENT bills support thesis discussion on:
- cash outflow and liability settlement in retail operations
- inter-organization payment reconciliation
- how payments materialize into cash flow metrics separate from invoice creation
- the role of manual payment and automatic receipt pairing in audit trails
