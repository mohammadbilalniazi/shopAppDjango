Selling Bill Documentation

1. Purpose

SELLING bills represent goods or services sold by an organization to another organization or customer. In the supermarket system, a SELLING bill is the revenue-generating transaction that reduces inventory and creates accounts receivable until payment is received.

2. Key Characteristics

- bill_type: SELLING
- creator organization: seller
- receiver organization: customer or counterparty organization when inter-organization billing is used
- inventory effect: stock quantity decreases
- price effect: selling_price updated on product details
- profit effect: profit is calculated as (selling price - purchase price) × quantity
- payment effect: payment amount may be partial or full, creating outstanding receivables

3. Opposite Transaction

SELLING bills are paired with PURCHASE bills for the counterparty organization. The pair ensures that one organization records a sale while the other records a purchase, maintaining balanced inter-organization accounting.

4. Financial Impact

- revenue is recognized by the seller as total bill amount
- accounts receivable grows by outstanding amount (total − payment)
- inventory decreases by sold quantity
- profit is generated when selling price exceeds cost basis

5. Thesis Relevance

For thesis discussion, SELLING bills are ideal to show:
- revenue recognition in retail operations
- inventory reduction and its effect on cost of goods sold
- how multi-organization billing preserves consistency between seller and purchaser
- the linkage between sales events and balance sheet/cash flow reporting
